ENVIRONMENTAL COMPATIBILITY & REALITY VALIDATION SCORER

1. THE REALITY VALIDATION CHALLENGE
-----------------------------------
A frequent issue in precision agricultural systems is input variance: users entering
ambient conditions (temperature, humidity, rainfall) that represent seasonal
averages, design assumptions, or errors, which differ significantly from current, 
ground-truth local weather conditions.

To resolve this, we implemented the Reality Validation Scorer.

2. MATH & LOGIC OF ALIGNMENT SCORING
------------------------------------
* Module: soil_ai_system/weather/analyzers/compatibility.py
* Formula:
  - We calculate:
    temp_diff = abs(user_temperature - live_temperature)
    hum_diff = abs(user_humidity - live_humidity)
  - Penalties are applied:
    temp_penalty = temp_diff * 4.5
    hum_penalty = hum_diff * 1.2
  - Overall Alignment Score:
    alignment_score = max(5, min(100, int(100 - temp_penalty - hum_penalty)))

* Interpretation Tiers:
  - [80% - 100%]: HIGH CONSISTENCY. Telemetry inputs reflect real-time conditions.
  - [50% - 79%]: MODERATE DIVERGENCE. Minor sensor offsets or seasonal transitions.
  - [5% - 49%]: CRITICAL ANOMALY. Flagged in warning red to indicate significant data conflict.

3. CONSISTENCY WARNINGS
-----------------------
When critical divergences are detected, explainable, scientific warnings are produced:
* "Temperature anomaly: Local sensors read X°C but manual input is Y°C."
* "Humidity divergence: Local relative humidity is X% vs manual Y%."
* "Water budget mismatch: Heavy rain context specified but local forecast is dry (<10mm next 7 days)."

4. ENVIRONMENTAL COMPATIBILITY SIGNALS
--------------------------------------
The system parses live humidity and short-term forecasts to offer positive compatibility indicators:
* High humidity (>75%): "Current atmospheric humidity strongly favors high-transpiration crops like paddy or jute."
* Heavy forecast (>50mm): "Rainfall forecast indicates robust water compatibility for high-hydration crop cycles."
* Low rainfall forecast: "Near-term forecast indicates minimal rainfall; irrigation adjustments advised."

5. ENVIRONMENTAL RISK MATRIX
----------------------------
Deterministic, non-hallucinatory risk levels (Low, Medium, High) are evaluated for:
* Drought Risk: Triggered by extreme heat (>35°C) and dry air (<30% humidity).
* Flood Sensitivity: Triggered by immediate rainfall or heavy 7-day accumulation (>120mm).
* Heat Stress: Triggered by temperatures above 34°C (Medium) or 40°C (High).
* Water Stress: Triggered by local drought, worsened if crop is strictly rain-fed.
* High Winds: Triggers crop mechanical lodging warnings if wind speeds exceed 25 km/h.
