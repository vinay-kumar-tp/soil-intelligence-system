PHASE 7C — SESSION MEMORY INTELLIGENCE REPORT
========================================================================
ANONYMOUS TIMELINE PERSISTENCE

1. SYSTEM DESIGN & PRIVACY BOUNDARIES
-------------------------------------
* Module: soil_ai_system/session_memory/memory_manager.py
* Active memory operates on a strictly in-memory session timeline cache.
* Zero personal identifiable information (PII) is captured.
* The cache strictly stores anonymized physical variables: N, P, K ratios, ambient
  temperatures, humidity rates, rainfall averages, crop suggestions, and isolated
  nutrient deficiencies over successive runs.
* Capacity limits are capped at exactly 10 historical records to keep the platform
  footprint low and lightweight.

2. RECUPERATION & DRIFT ALGORITHMS
----------------------------------
* Persistent Gaps:
  - If a chemical nutrient deficiency (such as Nitrogen) appears two or more times
    in successive runs, it is flagged as a "Recurring Soil Deficiency".
* Micro-Environmental Drifts:
  - Temperature Drift: Calculated as the delta between the current run's temperature
    and the session's first run temperature.
  - Drying Trend (Drought Drift): Triggered when temp drift is positive (> 2°C)
    paired with a declining humidity profile over the session lifecycle.
  - Rainfall trends: Classified dynamically into "drying", "wetting", or "stable".

3. OPTIMIZING DECISION CONTINUITY
---------------------------------
* Memory provides the critical bridge between isolated inference requests.
* By remembering prior states, recommendations adapt to long-term trends, advising
  the grower on transitional strategies (e.g. soil recovery cover cycles before
  resuming intensive cropping).
