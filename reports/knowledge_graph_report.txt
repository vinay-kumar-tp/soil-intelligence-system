CONNECTED RELATIONSHIP INTELLIGENCE

1. ARCHITECTURAL OVERVIEW
-------------------------
The Agronomic Knowledge Graph establishes a structured representation of key
agricultural nodes and semantic edges linking crops to their optimal environmental,
chemical, and physical parameters. Rather than relying solely on tabular
correlations, the platform now reasons through explicit domain-specific assertions.

2. GRAPH NODE SCHEMAS
---------------------
Our graph models relationships between nine core agronomic categories:
* Crops (e.g. Rice, Maize, Cotton, Groundnut, Sugarcane, Banana, Coconut)
* Soil Textures (e.g. Clay, Sandy, Loamy, Red Soil, Black Soil)
* Nutrient Deficiencies (N, P, K)
* Weather Conditions (Relative Humidity, Temperature, Rainfall)
* Irrigation Types (Rain-fed, Canal irrigation, Borewell, Drip irrigation)
* Agro-climatic Zones (Coastal, Delta, Semi-Arid, Western Ghats, Dry Plains)
* Environmental Risks (Drought, Flood, Heat Stress, Water Stress)
* Sustainability Factors (Water footprint, carbon footprint, soil carbon depletion)

3. GRAPH EDGE RULES (EXAMPLES)
------------------------------
* Rice Node:
  - prefers_soil -> clay, loamy
  - requires_humidity -> high
  - sensitive_to -> drought, water_stress
  - demands -> high Nitrogen
  - suited_for -> Coastal, Delta agro-climatic zones
* Groundnut Node:
  - prefers_soil -> sandy, red soil, loamy
  - suited_for -> Semi-Arid, Dry Plains, Coastal
  - nitrogen_fixer -> True (Acts as a natural replenisher)
  - sensitive_to -> drought, frost

4. RELATIONSHIP ENRICHMENT TRAVERSAL
------------------------------------
* Module: soil_ai_system/knowledge_graph/graph.py
* The traversal engine walks the graph edges during standard inference runs.
* It compares manual parameters against the selected crop's prefered edges.
* It outputs:
  - Matching edges (reaffirming indicators)
  - Conflicting edges (alerting penalties)
  - Sustainability factors (detailing the ecological footprint of the selected crop)
