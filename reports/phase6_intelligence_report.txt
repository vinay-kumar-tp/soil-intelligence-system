## 1. Subphase Implementations

### Phase 6A — Confidence-Aware Intelligence
- Implemented standard mapping of crop classification probabilities:
  * HIGH CONFIDENCE (>= 0.75): Strong environmental match.
  * MODERATE CONFIDENCE (>= 0.40): Partial environmental match; slight stability hazard.
  * LOW CONFIDENCE (< 0.40): Suboptimal environmental match; caution advised.
- Real-time confidence metrics are mapped to human-readable explanations.

### Phase 6B — Comparative Crop Reasoning (Top-K Index)
- Extracted the Top 3 crop suggestions from models' `predict_proba()` arrays.
- Structured an agronomic suitability evaluation checking:
  * Rainfall compatibility limits.
  * Temperature thresholds.
  * Atmospheric humidity limits.
  * Soil pH boundaries.
  * Current N-P-K nutrient status.
- Dynamically derives 3 core metrics per crop: Advantages (+), Risks (⚠), and Limiting Factors (✖).

### Phase 6C — Agronomic Narrative Engine
- Synthesizes dynamic, non-hallucinatory, scientifically grounded soil health summaries.
- Analyzes pH levels, climate bounds, and chemical deficiencies to formulate professional, human-readable paragraphs explaining exactly *why* a particular crop is recommended and what threats the soil faces.

### Phase 6D — Action Priority Mapping
- Categories:
  * HIGH PRIORITY (Immediate physical correctors like Agricultural Lime for high acidity or organic SOC humus integration).
  * MODERATE PRIORITY (Cultivation support like cover cropping or soil aeration).
  * OPTIONAL OPTIMIZATIONS (Long-term strategies like drip irrigation or rotational pulse farming).

### Phase 6E — Hybrid Research Intelligence Scoring
- Implemented a unified "Agronomic Intelligence Score" (0-100%).
- Blends neural model confidence, chemical deficiency weightings, and pH distance markers to represent long-term yield viability.
- Outputs detailed score justifications to improve transparency.
